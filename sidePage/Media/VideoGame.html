World of Warcraft

Ulike many other iterations, the trolls in Warcraft are intelligent protaganists. You can play and trolls along side many other races in a joint journey to the endgame. What ever that mey be these days, I havent played in a long time.

In Warcraft trolls look nothing like their norse counterparts. These guys have tusks, long hair and borrow more from haitian or carribean culture. practicing Voodoo and cannibalism.

These guys could have a whole blog just to themselves the warcraft lore is so rich and emersive. But that may be for another time.

Valheim

In this game you play a lone norse man/women venturing forth on a path set out for you by Odin himself. You battle many creature from myths one of them being Troll's

Here we will look at a few of the different types a little more closely. Valheim takes its image of trolls straight from norse myth. This type does not speak, they merely growl and try to smash you with their fists or giant logs.

This type is very close to it's source, though it ould do with a bith of finessing when it comes to their image. They look a little grey blobby sometimes. Though the game is still in it's most early of stages, so we have a lot to look forward to.

   The Witcher   

The witcher

The witcher again has another take on the Troll. In this game they are large being with large rock like protrusions on their backs. These trolls are huge, ugly, slow, dim witted and easy to fool, though if they were to catch you could squash to as easily as one squashed jelly.

The trolls in the witcher could be famously ferocious or they could be kind and almost wholesome. Some love to paint, others to sing, though they all bare the same appetite for man flesh if they can get it, or the odd shoe if that all they have.

All in all, i'd say these guys have enough of a connection to the old myths, to be directly referencing them.
