Troll Types

Because the myth of these creatures is handed down through tales spoken around camps or on long journeys. It can be difficult to get a great understanding of what, when where and why trolls came into being.

What we do know is that there are a few types of trolls spoken about in these tales some live high in the mountains. Others deeps in the darkest woods, others live far far deep down under ground in caves.

Here we will look at a few of the different types a little more closely.

Back to Top
   Mountain   

Mountain

The sleepiest of the bunch Mountain troll are very similar to forest Trolls in that they are strong, and have a remarkable connection to nature. They are generally rocky or mossy in appearance over the forest trolls rough bark like skin.

Though they could be mistaken for each other if you arent paying close attention. Not that you should ever bee in the situation where you would have to pay close attention to a troll.

Think of the smells!!

The rocky or mossy skin appears to be what allows mountain trolls to grow to such alarming sizes as it protects them from the sunlight. All they need to do is find themselves a nice area to curl up and they can sleep the daylight hours away unscathed.

This type can also generally blend into their surroundings, you may have even walked passed one and never known as they tend to appear like a small boulder or even a great hill.

A good example of a mountain troll is the one depicted in the movie trollhunter, it was as big as a small mountain. you can find an image of what they look like in our film and television section on our media page.

   Forest   

Forest

The first type is known as the forest or hill troll. They’re generally depicted as large, dumb, brutish creatures akin to a large neanderthal. These are the type described by tolkien in the hobbit, the charming fellows what wanted to eat hobbits and dwarves for dinner.

Fierce and strong they can uproot trees to use as clubs as well as being said to use their connections with nature or natural magics to be able to cause hurricanes and great avalanches.

These are also the trolls whose riddles you have to solve to cross a bridge, if you ever find yourself inside a fairytale and happen to be a goat!

Dunker is what would be considered a mountain Troll, you can learn about him in our stories section.

   Cave   

Cave

Unlike their forest-dwelling cousins, cave trolls live completely underground and are generally depicted as smaller than humans with a large round abdomen and short stubby arms and legs. These are more akin to the trolls that help Queen Elsa in Frozen.

In Norse mythology, however, they’re not generally friendly to humans. They use their connections with nature to baffle and deceive humans.

No matter the type of troll, they all have some characteristics in common. Aside from being unfriendly, they’re also generally depicted as stupid and dangerous. They may set clever riddles but they’re usually easy enough for humans to overcome.

   Sea   

Sea / water

Sea / Water Trolls are the most unknown of the trolls, similar to the scottish kelpie they resemble creatures covered in seaweed.
Living amongst the crashing waves and the sea foam, they lure their prey into the depths with songs.

These are said to be covered in fish like scales and have wide yawing mouths. Though naught much else is written about them.
